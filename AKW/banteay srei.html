History of BanTeay Srey

Banteay Srei was rediscovered by the French in 1914. However, it gained international attention when André Malraux, a French writer, and adventurer, attempted to steal several sculptures from the temple in 1923. He was caught, and the artifacts were returned, bringing global attention to the temple.

The temple underwent significant restoration efforts in the 1930s by the École française d'Extrême-Orient (EFEO). The restoration process included anastylosis, a method of reconstruction using original materials, which helped preserve the temple's intricate carvings.
